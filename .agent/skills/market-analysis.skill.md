## 1. Direct vs Indirect Competitors
- **Direct:** Solves the same problem for the same customer (e.g., Slack vs Teams).
- **Indirect:** Solves the same problem differently (e.g., Slack vs Email) or targets a different segment.
- **Replacement:** What is the user *currently* doing? (Excel sheets are often the biggest competitor).

## 3. Pricing Strategy Review
- **Cost-Plus:** Cost + Margin (Don't use for software).
- **Value-Based:** What is the value to the customer? (Preferred).
- **Competitor-Based:** Undercutting or Premium positioning.

## 4. SWOT Analysis
- **Strengths:** Internal advantages (Team, Tech, IP).
- **Weaknesses:** Internal disadvantages (Budget, Brand Awareness).
- **Opportunities:** External trends (AI boom, Remote work).
- **Threats:** External risks (Regulations, Big Tech entry).

## 5. "Blue Ocean" Strategy
Identify factors to:
- **Eliminate:** what industry takes for granted but adds no value?
- **Reduce:** what can be below industry standard?
- **Raise:** what should be well above industry standard?
- **Create:** what has never been offered?
